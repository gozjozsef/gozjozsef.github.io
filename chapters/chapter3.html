PCR Thermocycler

Run a PCR reaction by hitting the right temperature buttons in order: 95 °C denature → 55 °C anneal → 72 °C extend. Each cycle doubles your DNA — miss the timing and the cycle fails.

Cycle: 0 Copies: 1 Failures: 0/3

Why these temperatures? 95 °C breaks the hydrogen bonds between strands; 55 °C (the Tm) lets primers bind specifically; 72 °C is the optimum for Taq polymerase.
